Topic I · Foundations

What Is Intelligence, Artificial or Otherwise?

By Dr. I.M. Simeon · ABE Dept., FUTMINNA

Artificial intelligence is not science fiction. It is the technology already powering your phone's face unlock, your streaming recommendations, and the spam filter on your email. But what exactly is it? And how does it differ from human intelligence?

This first module explores the definition, scope, and historical milestones of AI — from Alan Turing's 1950 thought experiment to the rise of ChatGPT in 2022. You will understand the difference between Weak AI (what exists today) and the theoretical Strong AI (artificial general intelligence).

Topic II · Machine Learning

Teaching Machines to Learn Without Being Programmed

Core GET 307 Material · Week 1–4

Machine learning sits directly under AI in the technology hierarchy. Instead of writing rules explicitly, we feed data to an algorithm and let it discover the rules itself. This module breaks down the four major paradigms: supervised, unsupervised, semi-supervised, and reinforcement learning.

It then dives into Deep Learning — the multilayer neural network architecture that powers everything from image recognition to large language models — and culminates with Generative AI, the technology behind ChatGPT, DALL-E, and Midjourney.

Topic V · Model Performance

How Do You Know If Your Model Is Actually Good?

Accuracy alone is a liar. A model that says "No fraud" for every transaction in a dataset where 99% of transactions are genuine will achieve 99% accuracy — and miss every single fraudulent case. This module teaches you why you need Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC to truly measure a model's worth. It also covers hyperparameter tuning: how learning rate, batch size, epochs, and regularization strength can make or break your model's performance.
